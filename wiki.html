Employee Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy

Effective January 1, 2026  ·  Annual acknowledgement required  ·  Owned by Chief Compliance Officer

First National Bank is committed to the highest standards of ethical conduct and integrity in all business activities. This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral and ethical expectations for all employees in accordance with applicable federal banking regulations and supervisory guidance.1

1. Regulatory Framework & Obligations

All employees are subject to the ethical standards established by the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) under its Heightened Standards guidelines,1 as well as the Federal Reserve's compliance risk management expectations.2 Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and referral to the appropriate regulatory authority.

2. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and the Compliance Department. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Personal financial interests in entities with which the Bank does business
  • Outside employment or business activities
  • Gifts, entertainment, or other benefits received from third parties exceeding $50 in aggregate value per calendar year
  • Family or personal relationships with vendors, borrowers, or competitors
Annual Disclosure Required. All employees must complete the Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form annually by January 31. Failure to disclose is itself a violation of this policy.

3. Anti-Bribery & Gifts Policy

The Bank prohibits offering, giving, soliciting, or receiving any bribe or improper payment. This includes cash, excessive entertainment, or any benefit intended to influence a business decision. The Bank's policy aligns with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) for any international dealings.3

4. Confidential Information

Employees may not disclose confidential customer or Bank information outside of their authorized duties. This obligation persists after separation from employment. Customer financial information is protected under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)4 and unauthorized disclosure may carry civil and criminal penalties.

5. Reporting Violations

Employees who observe or suspect violations of this Code, applicable laws, or Bank policies are expected to report them through one of the following channels:

  • Direct supervisor or department head
  • Chief Compliance Officer (compliance@firstnational.bank)
  • Anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-0199 (available 24/7)
  • Whistleblower portal: ethics.firstnational.bank

Retaliation against employees who report concerns in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a violation of Bank policy and applicable law.2

Bank Secrecy Act & Anti-Money Laundering Compliance

Effective March 15, 2026  ·  Mandatory completion within 30 days of hire  ·  Owned by BSA Officer
⏳
Action required — Awaiting legal sign-off. This policy has been approved by the BSA Officer and Chief Compliance Officer. It is pending final review from A. Torres (General Counsel). Use the Approval Chain in the right panel to complete sign-off.
Mandatory Training. All employees must complete the BSA/AML training module in the LMS within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter. Non-completion triggers a compliance alert to your manager and the BSA Officer.

The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requires First National Bank to assist government agencies in detecting and preventing money laundering.1 Every employee, regardless of role, plays a part in our BSA/AML compliance program.

1. Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements

The Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule requires the Bank to collect and verify identifying information for all account holders and to identify beneficial owners of legal entity customers.2 Employees involved in account opening must:

  • Collect full legal name, date of birth, address, and government-issued ID for individuals
  • Identify all beneficial owners holding 25% or more equity interest in legal entities
  • Document and retain CDD records for a minimum of five years per 31 CFR § 1010.4301
  • Conduct enhanced due diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers including PEPs and MSBs

2. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)

Employees must report any transaction or pattern of activity that they know, suspect, or have reason to suspect involves funds from illegal activity, has no lawful purpose, or is designed to evade reporting requirements.3

Transaction TypeReporting ThresholdFiling DeadlineRetention
Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)$5,000+30 calendar days5 years
Currency Transaction Report (CTR)$10,000+ cash15 calendar days5 years
FBAR (customer advisory)$10,000+ foreign accountsApril 15 annually5 years

3. Structuring & Willful Blindness

"Structuring" — breaking transactions into smaller amounts to avoid CTR reporting thresholds — is a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324.1 Employees who assist in structuring, or who deliberately avoid learning about suspicious activity, may face personal criminal liability. Do not tip off customers when a SAR is filed; this is prohibited by law.

4. Red Flags for Money Laundering

  • Customers reluctant to provide identification or explanation for large cash transactions
  • Transactions inconsistent with a customer's known business or income level
  • Rapid movement of funds into and out of accounts (layering behavior)
  • Use of multiple accounts at different branches to aggregate cash deposits
  • Third parties paying off loans or providing deposits without a clear relationship

Customer Data Privacy & Information Security Policy

Effective January 1, 2026  ·  Governed by GLBA Regulation P  ·  Owned by Chief Privacy Officer

First National Bank is subject to the privacy provisions of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA),1 which govern how the Bank collects, uses, and protects nonpublic personal information (NPI) about its customers. All employees who access or handle customer information are bound by this policy.

1. What Is Nonpublic Personal Information (NPI)?

  • Account numbers, balances, and transaction history
  • Social Security Numbers and government-issued IDs
  • Credit scores, loan applications, and financial statements
  • Any information the customer provides to obtain a financial product or service
  • Information derived from any of the above

2. Privacy Notices & Opt-Out Rights

The Bank provides customers with an annual privacy notice as required by Regulation P.2 Customers have the right to opt out of information sharing with certain non-affiliated third parties. Employees who receive an opt-out request must process it within 30 days using the standard workflow in the CRM system.

Employee Access Principle. Access to customer NPI is granted on a need-to-know basis only. Accessing customer records outside your job function — even out of curiosity — is a policy violation and may constitute a GLBA breach.

3. Safeguards Program

The FTC Safeguards Rule requires the Bank to implement a comprehensive information security program.3 Employees are responsible for:

  • Locking workstations when away from their desk
  • Not sharing passwords or access credentials
  • Encrypting files containing NPI before email transmission
  • Reporting lost or stolen devices containing customer data within 1 hour
  • Shredding physical documents containing NPI before disposal

4. Data Breach Response

Employees who discover or suspect an unauthorized disclosure of customer NPI must immediately contact the Information Security team at security@firstnational.bank or call the 24/7 incident hotline at ext. 5500. The Bank is required to notify affected customers and regulators under applicable state law and agency guidance.4

IT Systems Access & Cybersecurity Onboarding

Effective February 1, 2026  ·  Aligned with FFIEC IT Handbook  ·  Owned by CISO

All employees are issued system credentials and access during the first week of employment. Access is granted according to the principle of least privilege — you receive the minimum access necessary to perform your job responsibilities, consistent with FFIEC IT examination guidance.1

1. Account Provisioning

New employee access is provisioned by IT within two business days of HR completion of onboarding paperwork. Your manager must submit an Access Request Form in ServiceNow, specifying each system and access level required. Access is role-based and mapped to your job function.

2. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA is mandatory for all systems, remote access, and email. NIST SP 800-63B2 guidance prohibits SMS-based OTP for high-security systems. The Bank uses hardware tokens (YubiKey) or authenticator apps (Microsoft Authenticator) for all privileged access.

Day 1 Action Required. You must activate MFA for your corporate email and VPN before the end of your first day. Contact the IT Help Desk at ext. 4400 if you need assistance.

3. Acceptable Use Policy

Bank systems are provided for business use. All activity on Bank systems is subject to monitoring and logging in accordance with the FFIEC IT Examination Handbook1 and NIST Cybersecurity Framework.3 Prohibited uses include:

  • Storing personal files or unauthorized software on Bank equipment
  • Accessing or downloading content unrelated to job duties
  • Using Bank systems to access personal social media or entertainment platforms
  • Installing any software not approved by IT Security
  • Forwarding Bank email or data to personal accounts

4. Access Termination

Upon separation from the Bank, all access is revoked within 4 hours of HR notification, consistent with NIST SP 800-53 AC-2 controls.4 Departing employees must return all Bank-issued equipment on or before their last day.

5. Phishing & Social Engineering Awareness

The Bank conducts quarterly simulated phishing campaigns. Employees who fail three or more simulations within a 12-month period will be required to complete mandatory cybersecurity awareness training. Report suspected phishing to phishing@firstnational.bank.
