Employee Code of Conduct & Ethics Policy

Effective Jan 1, 2026 · Annual acknowledgement required · Owned by Chief Compliance Officer

First National Bank is committed to the highest standards of ethical conduct and integrity in all business activities. This Code establishes behavioral expectations for all employees in accordance with applicable federal banking regulations.1

1. Regulatory Framework

All employees are subject to the ethical standards established by the OCC under its Heightened Standards guidelines1 and the Federal Reserve's compliance risk management expectations.2

2. Conflicts of Interest

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to their manager and the Compliance Department, including:

  • Personal financial interests in entities with which the Bank does business
  • Outside employment or business activities
  • Gifts or entertainment exceeding $50 aggregate value per calendar year
  • Family or personal relationships with vendors, borrowers, or competitors
Annual Disclosure Required. All employees must complete the Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form by January 31. Failure to disclose is itself a violation.

3. Reporting Violations

Employees who observe or suspect violations must report them via: direct supervisor, CCO (compliance@firstnational.bank), anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-0199, or the whistleblower portal. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is strictly prohibited.2

Regulatory References
1
OCC Guidelines Establishing Heightened Standards, 12 CFR Part 30, Appendix D (2014).
2
Federal Reserve SR 08-8: Compliance Risk Management Programs (2008).

Mandatory Training. All employees must complete the BSA/AML training module within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.

The Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) requires First National Bank to assist government agencies in detecting and preventing money laundering.1

1. Know Your Customer (KYC)

The Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Rule requires collection and verification of identifying information for all account holders and beneficial owners of legal entity customers.2 Employees must:

  • Collect full legal name, date of birth, address, and government-issued ID
  • Identify beneficial owners holding ≥25% equity in legal entities
  • Retain CDD records for a minimum of five years per 31 CFR § 1010.430
  • Conduct enhanced due diligence (EDD) for PEPs and MSBs

2. Suspicious Activity Reporting

Employees must report transactions they know, suspect, or have reason to suspect involve illegal funds or are designed to evade reporting requirements.3

Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)$5,000+30 days5 years

3. Structuring & Willful Blindness

Breaking transactions to avoid CTR thresholds ("structuring") is a federal crime under 31 U.S.C. § 5324.1 Do not tip off customers when a SAR is filed.

Customer Data Privacy & Information Security Policy

Effective Jan 1, 2026 · GLBA Regulation P · Chief Privacy Officer: S. Lee

First National Bank is subject to the privacy provisions of the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA),1 which govern how the Bank collects, uses, and protects nonpublic personal information (NPI) about customers.

1. Nonpublic Personal Information (NPI)

  • Account numbers, balances, and transaction history
  • Social Security Numbers and government-issued IDs
  • Credit scores, loan applications, and financial statements
  • Any information derived from the above

2. Safeguards Program

The FTC Safeguards Rule requires a comprehensive information security program.2 Employees must: lock workstations when away, never share credentials, encrypt NPI files before transmission, and report lost devices within 1 hour.

Need-to-Know Only. Accessing customer records outside your job function is a policy violation and may constitute a GLBA breach.
Regulatory References
1
Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, 15 U.S.C. §§ 6801–6809 (1999).
2
FTC Safeguards Rule, 16 CFR Part 314 (amended 2023).

IT Systems Access & Cybersecurity Onboarding

Effective Feb 1, 2026 · FFIEC IT Handbook · CISO: R. Kim

Access is granted on the principle of least privilege — you receive the minimum access necessary for your job, consistent with FFIEC IT guidance.1

1. Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is mandatory for all systems and remote access. Per NIST SP 800-63B,2 SMS-based OTP is prohibited for privileged access. The Bank uses YubiKey hardware tokens or Microsoft Authenticator.

Day 1 Required. Activate MFA for corporate email and VPN before end of your first day. IT Help Desk: ext. 4400.

2. Access Termination

Upon separation, all access is revoked within 4 hours of HR notification, per NIST SP 800-53 AC-2 controls.3

2

3
